# The Zing Taste Framework (v0.2, 2026-07-18)

How Zing answers "is this video good, and why" — never vibes, always
criteria with sources and confidence tiers. Built from deep-research round
R-A (105-agent adversarially-verified sweep: 23 sources fetched, 114 claims
extracted, 25 verified, 23 confirmed 3-0 or 2-1, 2 refuted) plus
confidence-flagged practitioner canon. S2 judgment scoring and S3 gap
reports cite THESE criteria by ID.

**Confidence tiers** (every criterion carries one):
- **T1 verified-platform** — platform's own live documentation, verified
  verbatim, adversarially confirmed 3-0.
- **T2 platform-statement** — platform's own claim, unverifiable
  methodology (e.g. internal studies).
- **T3 practitioner-consensus** — named experts with track records,
  multiple independent sources agreeing; not adversarially verified.
- **T4 single-opinion** — one credible voice. Usable in judgment prose,
  never as a scored criterion.

## Pillar H — The hook (first seconds)

- **H1 (T1).** Front-load: TikTok officially instructs hook within the
  first 6s and the content proposition within the first 3s; YouTube's
  Creator Playbook: attention-grab (verbal or visual) in the first 15s.
  [TikTok creative best practices](https://ads.tiktok.com/help/article/creative-best-practices) ·
  [YT Playbook](https://blog.youtube/creator-and-artist-stories/youtube-creator-playbook-tips-first-15/)
- **H2 (T1).** Structural hooks YouTube endorses by name: **open loop**
  (tease the payoff) and **cold open** (content before branding — "no
  flashy intro"). [YT Playbook pt.1](https://blog.youtube/news-and-events/this-is-first-of-series-of-posts/)
- **H3 (T1).** Hook is partly a **packaging-congruence** effect: YouTube
  attributes high intro retention to the opening matching the
  thumbnail/title expectation — score hook-vs-promise match, not just
  spectacle. [YT retention docs](https://support.google.com/youtube/answer/9314415)
- **H4 (T2).** TikTok claims 90% of ad recall impact lands in the first 6s
  (internal 2020 analysis, methodology unpublished; split-vote 2-1).
  [source](https://ads.tiktok.com/business/en/blog/creative-best-practices-top-performing-ads)
- **H5 (T1, context).** A declining retention curve is the documented
  normal — the argument for moving the best material earlier. YouTube's
  measured "Intro" window is 30s (long-form analytics); short-form advice
  clusters at 1–6s. Different surfaces, score per-format.

**Zing measures:** shots/words/captions in 0–3s (Breakdown hook window),
keyframes for visual-hook judgment. **Zing judges:** hook type, hook-promise
congruence, time-to-proposition.

## Pillar C — Hold to completion (the universal ranking currency)

- **C1 (T1, strongest finding of the round).** All three platforms
  independently name completion/watch-through as first-order ranking
  signal: TikTok weights per-video watch time "more heavily than other
  factors" and predicts finish/skip; Instagram's most-important Reels
  predictions: reshare, watch-all-the-way-through, like, go-to-audio;
  YouTube Shorts ranks on view-choice rate ("viewed vs swiped away"),
  average view duration, average % viewed + likes and post-watch surveys.
  [TikTok transparency](https://www.tiktok.com/transparency/en/recommendation-system) ·
  [TikTok newsroom](https://newsroom.tiktok.com/en-us/how-tiktok-recommends-videos-for-you) ·
  [IG ranking explained](https://about.instagram.com/blog/announcements/instagram-ranking-explained) ·
  [YT Shorts discovery](https://support.google.com/youtube/answer/11914225?hl=en-GB&co=YOUTUBE._YTVideoType%3Dshorts)
- **C2 (T1).** Shares are the strongest secondary signal (top-weighted on
  Reels; predicted on TikTok).
- **C3 (T1).** TikTok's composite scoring predicts like/share/comment/skip/
  finish/favorite/follow/watch-duration/soundtrack-tap; leaked Algo-101 doc
  (NYT 2021, authenticated) corroborates playtime dominance.

**Zing measures:** duration discipline, pacing curve, dead-air. **Zing
judges:** where a viewer would swipe (retention-risk moments), payoff
delivery on the hook's promise.

## Pillar P — Production floor (table stakes the platforms police)

- **P1 (T1).** TikTok baseline: sound/music present, vertical 9:16, ≥720p,
  key content inside the UI safe zone. (Exact safe-zone pixel margins NOT
  yet verified — R-2 item.) [source](https://ads.tiktok.com/help/article/creative-best-practices)
- **P2 (T1).** Instagram explicitly downranks: low-resolution, third-party
  watermarks (own logo is fine), muted reels, borders, majority-text,
  reposts. A concrete checkable penalty list.
  [IG ranking explained](https://about.instagram.com/blog/announcements/instagram-ranking-explained)

**Zing measures:** resolution/aspect/audio-presence directly; caption
coverage; (safe-zone check once R-2 lands margins).

## Pillar A — Anti-slop (the line Zing exists to hold)

- **A1 (T1).** YouTube's monetization policy is the strongest primary-source
  slop definition: content must "not be mass-produced, generic, repetitive,
  or manipulative"; named disqualified formats include image slideshows,
  templated storylines, scrolling text, and template-driven AI video giving
  the impression of mass production.
  [YT monetization policies](https://support.google.com/youtube/answer/1311392?hl=en)
- **A2 (T1).** The dividing line is demonstrable human creative vision, not
  AI use: automated tools are fine only when "the final product must still
  demonstrate your creative vision." This IS Zing's design thesis — the
  director loop (your footage, your gaps, your shot prompts) is what keeps
  output on the right side of it.
- **A3 (refuted — do not cite).** Animoto's "83% of consumers can spot AI
  video" and its giveaway list failed adversarial verification (1-2, 0-3).
  No verified perceptual data on AI-video tells exists yet; R-2 hunts
  academic HCI literature.

## Pillar E — Editing craft (T3 practitioner canon; R-2 verification pending)

Not adversarially verified this round — flagged T3, multiple independent
sources each, quoted from named practitioners:

- **E1 (T3).** Walter Murch's Rule of Six (In the Blink of an Eye, p.18) —
  a ready-made weighted cut rubric: Emotion 51%, Story 23%, Rhythm 10%,
  Eye-trace 7%, 2D screen plane 5%, 3D spatial continuity 4%. Emotion alone
  outweighs everything else combined; continuity is the LEAST important.
  (6 independent secondary sources agree on the weights.)
- **E2 (T3).** Invisible editing at the top tier: Apple keynote videos hide
  cuts so well they can't be found without frame-scrubbing — great tech
  launch videos conceal their edits rather than showcase them.
- **E3 (T3).** Neistat vlog grammar: cold open → setup hint → content →
  wrap; mini-arcs inside the video; the "Neistat cut-off" — cut mid-sentence
  the moment the viewer can complete the thought; time-lapse to kill dead
  time. Pace by trusting the audience.
- **E4 (T3).** Packaging-first (Paddy Galloway): idea/title/thumbnail —
  not production polish — drives order-of-magnitude view differences; top
  creators spend ~30% of time on ideation. Consistent with H3's
  packaging-congruence (T1).

## Rubric skeleton v0 (what S2 judgment scores, per genre)

Each scored 1–5 with a cited criterion ID and a measurement reference:
1. Hook speed & type (H1, H2) — time-to-proposition, hook classification
2. Hook-promise congruence (H3, E4)
3. Retention shape (C1) — pacing curve vs genre norm, dead-air, payoff
4. Cut quality (E1) — motivated cuts (emotion/story) vs metronome cutting
5. Caption craft (P2 + measured caption style match)
6. Sound (P1) — speech clarity, music use, (ducking norms: R-2)
7. Production floor (P1, P2) — pass/fail checklist
8. Human vision / anti-slop (A1, A2) — is there an original perspective a
   template couldn't produce?
