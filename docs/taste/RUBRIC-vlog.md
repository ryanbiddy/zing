# Vlog Rubric (v1.0, 2026-07-18)

This rubric evaluates the "Vlog" genre (e.g., travel vlogs, daily diaries, and documentary-style visual journalism). Scoring is on a 1–5 scale per criterion.

---

## Rubric Breakdown

### G-VL-1: Relatable or Active Hook (Weight: 20%)
* **Description:** An active opening showing a journey, movement, or a candid personal statement within the first 6 seconds, avoiding intro titles or logos.
* **Zing Measures:**
  * Motion vector variance in the first 0–3s (target: high camera movement/action).
  * Time-to-vocal-activation (target: under 2s).
* **AI Judges:**
  * Hook structure type (e.g., active journey, daily mission, low-energy greeting).
  * Efficacy: is the setup intriguing and relatable?
* **Confidence Tier:** T1 (YouTube Playbook / TikTok rules) | T3 (Practitioner teardowns).
* **Source Trace:**
  * Framework: H1, H2.
  * Teardown: Casey Neistat (active travel mission), Emma Chamberlain (low-energy greeting in car/bed).

### G-VL-2: Rhythmic & Musical Pacing (Weight: 15%)
* **Description:** Cut rhythm synced to the soundtrack tempo or conversational speed. Uses timelapses to compress dead time, and mid-sentence cuts to maintain pace.
* **Zing Measures:**
  * Alignment of visual cuts with audio beat peaks.
  * Average cut interval.
  * Frame rate variance (timelapse speed-ups).
* **AI Judges:**
  * Pacing: are cuts motivated by musical changes or comedic beats?
* **Confidence Tier:** T3 (Walter Murch's Rule of Six / Neistat vlog grammar / Emma Chamberlain edits).
* **Source Trace:**
  * Framework: E1, E3.
  * Teardown: Casey Neistat (rhythmic musical cuts), Emma Chamberlain (mid-phrase jump cuts).

### G-VL-3: Character-Driven Camera Interaction (Weight: 15%)
* **Description:** Breaking the fourth wall by showing camera setup, walking shots, or direct face-to-lens eye contact. The camera functions as a character in the scene.
* **Zing Measures:**
  * Face detection duration percentage across the timeline.
  * Camera motion vectors (handheld movement vs static shots).
* **AI Judges:**
  * Visual engagement: does the direct address feel natural and engaging?
  * Physical integration: does the creator show the physical process of filming?
* **Confidence Tier:** T3 (Practitioner-consensus).
* **Source Trace:**
  * Teardown: Casey Neistat (setting up tripod/walking past/retrieving), Emma Chamberlain (FaceTime-like car/bed framing).

### G-VL-4: Immersive Soundscapes & Sonic Punctuation (Weight: 15%)
* **Description:** Rich ambient audio environments (traffic, wind, footsteps) mixed with comedic sound effects (buzzer, record scratch, vocal sighs) used as punctuation.
* **Zing Measures:**
  * Sound track complexity (number of layered audio channels).
  * Non-speech sound spikes synced with visual changes.
* **AI Judges:**
  * Sound design integration: do environmental ambient tracks and comedic sound cues enhance the setting?
* **Confidence Tier:** T1 (TikTok soundtrack dominance) | T3 (Practitioner-consensus).
* **Source Trace:**
  * Framework: P1.
  * Teardown: Casey Neistat (foley amplification), Johnny Harris (geopolitical paper/pencil soundscapes).

### G-VL-5: Narrative Progression & Mini-Arcs (Weight: 10%)
* **Description:** Structured daily journey featuring a setup, obstacles, climax, and a final philosophical or humorous takeaway.
* **Zing Measures:**
  * Total duration.
  * Segment durations (visual shifts).
* **AI Judges:**
  * Narrative structure: does the video present a coherent journey with a clear setup, climax, and resolution?
* **Confidence Tier:** T3 (Practitioner-consensus).
* **Source Trace:**
  * Framework: E3.
  * Teardown: Casey Neistat (mission -> obstacle -> resolution -> takeaway).

### G-VL-6: Caption & Text Integration (Weight: 10%)
* **Description:** Simple, stylized lower thirds for location/time or handwritten text overlays for internal thoughts, avoiding flashing colors or block fonts.
* **Zing Measures:**
  * Subtitle text area and contrast ratio against background video.
* **AI Judges:**
  * Graphic styling: evaluates readability and suitability of labels/annotations.
* **Confidence Tier:** T1 (Instagram caption checks) | T3 (Practitioner-consensus).
* **Source Trace:**
  * Framework: P2.
  * Teardown: Emma Chamberlain (ironic subtitles), Casey Neistat (bold location stamps), Johnny Harris (document labels).

### G-VL-7: Authenticity & Anti-Slop (Weight: 15%)
* **Description:** Authentic presentation that avoids generic travel templates or over-polished corporate looks. Embraces physical flaws, mistakes, and raw environments.
* **Zing Measures:**
  * Filter/LUT repetition check.
  * Detection of standard transition template fingerprints.
* **AI Judges:**
  * Authenticity: does the video feel like a real human experience, or an over-edited template?
* **Confidence Tier:** T1 (YouTube monetization guidelines) | T3 (Practitioner-consensus).
* **Source Trace:**
  * Framework: A1, A2.
  * Teardown: Emma Chamberlain (raw yawns/sighs/awkward pauses), Casey Neistat (rough raw textures).
