## 1. Tech Genre

### Marques Brownlee (MKBHD)
* **Source:** [YouTube Channel](https://www.youtube.com/@mkbhd) | [TikTok Profile](https://www.tiktok.com/@mkbhd) | [Waveform Podcast](https://www.youtube.com/@Waveform)
* **Hook Construction:**
  * **YouTube:** Scripted statement of central tension spoken over an ultra-close macro shot of the device. For example: "The phone you should not buy" `[verified-data]`.
  * **TikTok:** Immediate visual pattern interrupt. He holds the physical device close to the lens and delivers a quick opinion. Cuts occur within 1.5 seconds `[practitioner-consensus]`.
* **Cut Rhythm:**
  * **YouTube:** Slow, structured pacing. The visual cuts average 3 to 5 seconds, using robot-controlled camera movements (RED/Arri rigs) `[practitioner-consensus]`.
  * **TikTok:** Brisk pacing. Cuts average 1.5 to 2.5 seconds, alternating between talking-head reactions and hand-held macro details `[practitioner-consensus]`.
* **Caption Style:**
  * **YouTube:** No verbal captions. High-contrast lower-thirds display hardware specs using minimal grey and white blocks `[verified-data]`.
  * **TikTok:** Bold, center-aligned, word-timed subtitles. Uses custom fonts with high contrast to keep attention `[practitioner-consensus]`.
* **Sound Use:**
  * **YouTube:** Low-volume hip-hop beats. Vocals are recorded via high-end studio shotgun mics to ensure high clarity `[verified-data]`.
  * **TikTok:** Upbeat, trending audio loops mixed behind the vocals `[practitioner-consensus]`.
* **Structure Beats:** (1) Setup tension, (2) design check, (3) day-to-day use, (4) performance test, and (5) value verdict.
* **The ONE Thing:** The clean studio look. High-end camera movements and expert studio lighting make the devices look premium `[practitioner-consensus]`.

* **Structure Beats:** (1) Crazy project introduction, (2) component assembly, (3) testing and troubleshooting, and (4) final results.

## 2. Comedy Genre

### CalebCity
* **Source:** [YouTube Channel](https://www.youtube.com/@CalebCity) | [TikTok Profile](https://www.tiktok.com/@calebcity)
* **Hook Construction:**
  * **YouTube:** Starts immediately with a skit premise. For example: "When the hero is too late." No intro card is used `[verified-data]`.
  * **TikTok:** Immediate dialogue establishing the conflict within the first 2 seconds `[practitioner-consensus]`.
* **Cut Rhythm:**
  * **YouTube:** Fast, reaction-based pacing. Cuts occur every 1 to 3 seconds, alternating between different characters (all played by Caleb) to build comedic timing `[practitioner-consensus]`.
  * **TikTok:** Extremely fast. Cuts average under 1.5 seconds, removing all silence to keep the dialogue snappy `[practitioner-consensus]`.
* **Caption Style:**
  * **YouTube:** No captions.
  * **TikTok:** Bold, yellow, or white phrase subtitles centered on screen `[practitioner-consensus]`.
* **Sound Use:**
  * **YouTube:** Heavy use of anime or cartoon sound effects (punch sounds, whooshes, dramatic pauses) to emphasize physical comedy `[practitioner-consensus]`.
  * **TikTok:** Quick sound cues synced with exaggerated facial reactions `[practitioner-consensus]`.
* **Structure Beats:** (1) Premise setup, (2) escalation of the conflict, (3) physical confrontation or climax, and (4) abrupt comedic exit.
* **The ONE Thing:** Exaggerated physical comedy. The editing uses fast cuts and cartoon sound effects to highlight his facial expressions and movement `[practitioner-consensus]`.

### Ryan George
* **Source:** [YouTube Channel](https://www.youtube.com/@ryangeorge) | [TikTok Profile](https://www.tiktok.com/@ryangeorge)
* **Hook Construction:**
  * **YouTube:** Direct address to the camera introducing a character or a pitch meeting setup. For example: "So, you have a movie for me?" `[verified-data]`.
  * **TikTok:** Immediate entry into a sketch dialogue, starting with a question `[practitioner-consensus]`.
* **Cut Rhythm:**
  * **YouTube:** Structured back-and-forth pacing. Cuts occur exactly at the end of each spoken line (average 1.5 to 3 seconds) to switch between characters `[practitioner-consensus]`.
  * **TikTok:** Tighter dialogue cuts. Removes any gaps between spoken lines to maintain a high tempo `[practitioner-consensus]`.
* **Caption Style:**
  * **YouTube:** No captions.
  * **TikTok:** High-contrast, clean sans-serif text overlays for key dialogue terms `[practitioner-consensus]`.
* **Sound Use:**
  * **YouTube:** Quiet or no background music during dialogue. Focuses entirely on vocal delivery and catchphrases (e.g., "super easy, barely an inconvenience") `[verified-data]`.
  * **TikTok:** Minimal ambient sound, keeping the vocal track clean and prominent `[practitioner-consensus]`.
* **Structure Beats:** (1) Sketch premise, (2) logic escalation, (3) catchphrase delivery, and (4) sudden ending.
* **The ONE Thing:** The structured character switch. The rhythm of his cuts creates a natural dialogue flow despite him playing every character `[practitioner-consensus]`.
